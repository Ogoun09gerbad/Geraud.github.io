02

Master's Thesis

Master 2 · Functional Analysis IMSP · Benin Defended 26/09/2025

Comportement asymptotique et profils autosemblables
des solutions de l'équation de la chaleur dans ℝᴺ

We study the long-time asymptotic behavior of solutions to the Cauchy problem for the heat equation in ℝᴺ. Using the Fourier transform and semigroup theory, we establish that every integrable solution converges, as t → ∞, towards a self-similar Gaussian profile of the same mass — an analogue of the Central Limit Theorem at the PDE level.


We then derive precise convergence rates of order t⁻¹/² and t⁻¹ under finite first- and second-moment conditions, and prove their optimality through explicit counterexamples. The work unifies classical PDE theory with harmonic analysis and probability.
